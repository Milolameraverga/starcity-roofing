Common Roofing Problems in East El Paso

  • Aging shingles on 1980s homes: A large portion of East El Paso housing was built 35–45 years ago. Many of these roofs have already been re-shingled once and are showing a second wear cycle, curling edges, heavy granule loss, and decking softness beneath.
  • Hail impact damage: East El Paso sees frequent spring and storm-season hail. Small pitting visible from the ground can signal serious asphalt bruising that undermines waterproofing long-term. Our inspections check shingle impact points, granule displacement, and underlayment exposure.
  • Poor attic ventilation: Many East El Paso homes have inadequate ridge or soffit ventilation, which superheats the underside of shingles and dramatically shortens their life. We assess ventilation as part of every inspection at no charge.
  • UV-cracked pipe boot flashings: Rubber pipe boot gaskets are one of the most common sources of leaks in El Paso. The intense UV cracks the rubber in as little as 10 years. If you have a mystery drip near a bathroom, this is often the culprit.

East El Paso Roofing, FAQ

How do I know if I need a repair or a full replacement? +
We determine this during a free on-site inspection. If damage is isolated, the roof is under 15 years old, and overall shingle condition is good, a repair makes sense. If damage is widespread, the roof is 20+ years old, or you've had multiple repairs recently, replacement is typically the better investment. We'll give you our honest recommendation, no upsell pressure.
What roofing materials do you install in East El Paso? +
As an Owens Corning Preferred Contractor, we primarily install Duration Series shingles, Energy Star rated, Class 4 impact resistance, ideal for El Paso's hail and UV exposure. We also carry Duration COOL shingles for homeowners focused on cooling cost reduction. All installs include synthetic underlayment and ice and water shield at valleys and penetrations.
Do you help with insurance claims in East El Paso? +
Yes, this is one of our strengths. After a storm, we inspect, photograph all damage, document affected areas, and provide a full report for your insurance company. We can meet with your adjuster on-site. Many East El Paso homeowners have received full or near-full claim coverage with our documentation.
How long does roof replacement take in East El Paso? +
Most single-family homes in East El Paso are completed in one full day. Larger homes or those with significant decking damage may take two days. We schedule so crews complete the job from start to finish, we don't leave homes exposed overnight if we can help it.
