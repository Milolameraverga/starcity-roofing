Why Horizon City Roofs Need Attention

  • Builder-grade shingles hitting their limit: Many Horizon City subdivisions were built with standard 3-tab or entry-level architectural shingles to hit a price point. At 15–20 years old in El Paso's UV environment, these systems are often at the end of their effective life, even if they haven't started leaking yet.
  • Hail exposure: Horizon City's location east of the Franklin Mountains puts it in the path of storms that develop over Texas and move west. Hail events are frequent during the spring (March–May) and storm season (July–September), and impact damage on builder-grade shingles is often more extensive than homeowners realize.
  • Expansion and contraction cycling: Horizon City's desert climate creates dramatic daily temperature swings, up to 40°F in a single day. This thermal cycling causes roofing materials to expand and contract repeatedly, loosening fasteners, cracking sealant at penetrations, and accelerating shingle brittleness over time.
  • HOA considerations: Many Horizon City communities have HOAs with specific requirements for roofing materials, colors, and contractor qualifications. We know these approval processes and handle the documentation so you don't have to chase it yourself.

My Horizon City home was built in 2005, is it time to replace the roof? +
A 2005 home with original builder-grade shingles is approaching 20 years old in El Paso's harsh UV environment, which is right at the edge of the typical service life for standard architectural shingles in this climate. It's not necessarily time yet, but a free inspection will reveal the actual condition. Look for: heavy granule loss in gutters, cracked or curling edges, and any signs of interior moisture. Call us and we'll tell you honestly what we see.
Will my Horizon City HOA approve Star City Roofing? +

We've helped homeowners navigate HOA approval processes throughout the El Paso area.
